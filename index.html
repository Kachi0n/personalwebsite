Street Fighter: Snap Edition

Created my own version of Street Fighter with 2 modes, and created working video game controllers.

  • Tech Stack: Snap, Arduino.
  • Created a Player vs Player level, and a Player vs AI level

EquiTea

An app dedicated to helping users find restaurants based on their social causes they are interested in.

  • Tech Stack: Java, Android Studio
  • This was a project for my User Interface and Design class. I got to learn Human Computer Interaction and how to create apps for social justice causes.
  • The (Really Cool) Origin Story: In Summer 2020, as BSU Communications Director, I raised $50,000 and secured resources for Black student organizations and housing-insecure students at UC Berkeley. Witnessing how local businesses rallied to support our cause inspired me to pitch the idea of EquiTea to our team, an app that connects users with inclusive restaurants aligned with their values and causes they want to support—whether it’s accessibility, LGBTQ+ acceptance, and more.

Interactive Feature for Crown Channel

Led the R&D of a Twitch extension for Crown Channel that turns audience interactions into personalized ways to support, or sabotage their favorite streamers in our Fight Night live show.

  • Tech Stack: AWS, Twitch API, Typescript, React
  • Designed the "Channel Points Redemption Tracker," enabling audiences to redeem points to sabotage or support streamers (e.g., low brightness, one-handed play).
  • Initially launched midway through Fight Night Season 1, its success led to adaptations for future seasons, including "Vibe Check" and brand integrations with Intel and EA.
  • This was my first opportunity to explore experimental, interactive, and personalized storytelling!
  • Fun Fact: My first manager was 1 of 4 creators of Madden, and my later manager was a lead game developer at Telltale Games—one of my favorite studios and a pioneer in decision-based storytelling. Their guidance was invaluable for me as a gamer and fan of adaptive storytelling.
