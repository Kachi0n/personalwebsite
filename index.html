About Me

Hi, I’m Kachi Onyeador! I’m passionate about creating immersive, interactive experiences that combine technology, storytelling, and design. With a background in cognitive science, computer science, and theater, I bring a unique interdisciplinary approach to my work, blending creativity with technical innovation.

I’ve worked on projects ranging from adaptive storytelling in live shows to AI-driven solutions for education and well-being. My mission is to design experiences that not only captivate but also make a meaningful impact. I’m particularly fascinated by the emerging intersections of media psychology and politics, especially how new technologies shape our perceptions and behaviors in a rapidly changing world.

When I’m not working, I’m diving into the world of full-dive VR and geeking out about decision-based and story-driven games. I’m a gamer at heart and love collecting video games and consoles—it’s one of my favorite ways to explore how storytelling evolves across platforms. Outside of gaming, I’m into hiking, tennis, and spending time with my mini goldendoodle, Obi. Lately, I’ve been learning Spanish and Igbo while traveling to new places—whether it’s day trips around California or adventures across the globe.

Feel free to explore my work or reach out—I’d love to connect and talk about the future of interactive media, immersive storytelling, and the role of technology in our lives!

Robotic Prosthetic Arm

A low-cost prosthetic arm that uses neck movements and light sensors to help users grab and manipulate objects without hand controls.

Assistive Technology Accessibility Hardware
  • Tech Stack: Arduino, Servo Motors, Photoresistors, Custom Circuit Design, PVC Construction
  • Created an innovative two-finger grip mechanism that could handle diverse objects - from small nuts and bolts to larger items - while keeping production costs under $40 to ensure accessibility.
  • This was my first opportunity to explore assistive technology and human-centered design, showing me how engineering can directly impact people's lives!
  • What started as a MESA competition project opened my eyes to adaptive technology design. Learning that high-end prosthetics can cost tens of thousands of dollars motivated our team to prove that effective solutions could be built affordably.
